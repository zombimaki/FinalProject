Random Forest Predictive Model

Understanding which neighborhoods will experience crime is critical for public safety. Forecasting where crime is most likely to occur enables police to concentrate their limited resources in the neighborhoods that need it most. This approach used two Random Forest models designed to identify the most impactful features impacting crime incidents neighborhoods. The first model used all 87 Minneapolis neighborhoods. The second focused on the Downtown West neighborhood which historically has the highest incidents of crime every year and is consistently red on the incident heatmaps reflected on this website.


Due to the categorial nature of the data in the models, two years (2018-2019) of data and 37,380 crime records were analyzed.

Model 1: All Neighborhoods

Model 1 is designed to help identify the neighbors crimes will most likely occur. The output (label) of this Random Forest model are the 87 Minneapolis neighborhoods.


The input features analyzed were:


  • Precinct
  • Offense
  • UCR Code
  • Incident Time
  • Month
  • Week
  • Day of Month


    • The top ten features are reflected below descending by order of importance. Time (day, month, year), precinct location, and offense (theft) are the most impactful features.

    This infomation may be helpful for police staffing.

    Model 1 Scores


    The results of the model had a low accuracy. The training data accounted for 57% of the variation, but when the model was applied to the testing data, the score was only 12%.

    This intitial fit confirmed the challenges with predicting crime in neighborhoods with the limited available data.



    After hypertuning the model, an insignficant improvement (0.0007) was made in the score of the model.




    Model 2: Downtown West


    Model 2 focused on one neighborhood which had the highest rate of crime to determine which features could be used to predict an incident.

    The same features were used to predict incidents as were used in Model 1.


    The top six features are reflected below descending by order of importance. As in the first model, Day, Month, Day of Week, and Offense (Theft - UCR Code 7) were impactful. This model also showed the the time period of 1 AM was impactful.

    Model 2 Scores


    Both the training and testing data had high scores. The training data created a model with 99% fit. This strong fit was reflected when the model was tested with a score of 90%.

    This model will be much more useful in making more precise forecasts of crime in the Downtown-West neighborhood




    After hypertuning the model, the model score improved an insignificant amount (0.01).




    Summary

    With limitied precision, the two neighborhood models the importance of police staffing at the right place and time. Model 2 provided useful information that could be leverage by police when chosing times, day of week, month and day to patrol.
